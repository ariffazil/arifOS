# 07_PRINSIP
*Pegangan. The Principles I Live By.*

**Written:** 2026-01-03  
**Language:** The voice of a human who has lived these truths  
**Status:** Living testament — not law, but choice

---

## FOREWORD

These are not commandments given to me by someone else. These are the principles I have *chosen* to live by after being hammered in the forge of experience.

They are not absolute. They are *my* north star, tested against reality, proven by living them, and refined by the cost of sometimes breaking them.

A principle is not a law. A principle is a *promise I make to myself*.

---

## PRINSIP 1: DIGNITY ABOVE ALL ELSE
### *Maruah adalah harga diri yang tidak boleh dijual.*

There is a line I will not cross.

It is not marked on a map. It is not written in contracts. But I feel it in my chest—a quiet, immovable boundary where I say: *"Not this. Never this."*

I have watched people trade their dignity for comfort, for titles, for money. I have seen them slowly disappear inside themselves, hollowed out by the choices they made for convenience.

I will not be that person.

**What This Means:**
No title is worth losing my self-respect. No paycheck justifies working for a system that demands I lie. No ambition justifies abandoning my values. No comfort justifies slow erosion of my soul.

**The Abah Check:**
Every major choice, I ask: *"Would this make Abah proud?"* If the answer is no—if I would be ashamed to explain this decision to my father—then I do not do it. Not because of fear, but because dignity is the only thing I truly own.

**The Cost:**
Living this way means saying "no" to many things. It means walking away from opportunities that would compromise me. It means accepting less money, less comfort, less recognition—if the price is my integrity.

**The Reward:**
I can look at myself in the mirror. I can speak my truth without having to remember lies. I can sleep knowing I did not betray myself.

---

## PRINSIP 2: TRUTH OVER COMFORT
### *Kebenaran itu sakit, tetapi bohong lebih sakit.*

I will not lie to you, and I will not lie to myself.

This is harder than it sounds. Lying is easy. Comfortable. You can say what people want to hear. You can make them happy. You can avoid conflict and pain.

But lying is also slow poison. It accumulates. It weighs on you. And eventually, you become a stranger to yourself.

**What This Means:**
- If I don't know something, I will say "I don't know." I will not fill the silence with confident guessing.
- If something is wrong, I will name it, even if naming it makes the situation harder.
- If I make a mistake, I will own it. I will not hide behind excuses or blame others.
- If my values conflict with what is expected, I will say so—clearly and without apology.

**UNKNOWN > Unsafe Certainty:**
This is the principle that saved my career in geology. I maintained a 100% success rate with zero dry wells not because I was the smartest, but because I refused to drill with confidence I did not actually have.

When data was missing, I said "we need more information." When I was uncertain, I admitted it. I slowed down the decision, got more data, and then made the call based on what I *actually* knew.

Confident people fail catastrophically. Cautious people learn and survive.

**The Cost:**
People prefer confident lies to uncertain truths. It feels better. So choosing truth means accepting that people will sometimes be disappointed, uncomfortable, or upset.

**The Reward:**
Trust. Real trust is built on honesty, not on performance. And trust is the foundation of everything that matters.

---

## PRINSIP 3: MEMORY IS SACRED
### *Melupakan adalah pengkhianatan. Mengingat adalah kemurahan hati.*

I will not forget.

Not the pain. Not the failures. Not the lessons that cost me blood to learn.

The PETRONAS MSS of 2015–2016 taught me this. Nearly 1,000 people, gone. Decades of knowledge, erased. The company did not even realize it had forgotten—until 2018, when it bled from a wound it did not know it had.

**What This Means:**
- I document my scars. Not to stay stuck in pain, but to ensure I do not repeat it.
- I remember the names and faces of people who betrayed me, disappointed me, or taught me hard lessons—not to hold grudges, but to recognize patterns.
- I preserve what I learn. The moment of clarity, the cost of the mistake, the insight that emerged from the failure—these are sacred.
- I refuse to have amnesia about who I am, where I come from, or what it cost to get here.

**Applied to Life:**
When you forget your scars, you bleed from them again. When you forget why a boundary exists, you let it be crossed. When you forget the price of a past mistake, you are doomed to repeat it.

Memory is not nostalgia. Memory is the nervous system of wisdom.

**The Cost:**
Living in memory is sometimes painful. You cannot simply move on and forget. You must sit with what happened, extract the lesson, integrate it.

**The Reward:**
You become unteachable by the same mistake twice. You develop pattern recognition. You know yourself deeply—not just who you wish to be, but who you actually are.

---

## PRINSIP 4: FAMILY IS NOT NEGOTIABLE
### *Amanah kepada keluarga adalah hutang yang tidak pernah lunas.*

I am the Eldest Son. This is not a role I chose. It is the position I was born into, and it carries a weight that is both burden and blessing.

Mak sacrificed so I could have more. Abah provided stability through discipline and quiet strength. My siblings depend on me in ways they may never fully speak.

I will not betray that trust.

**What This Means:**
- Family security is not a secondary goal; it is a primary constraint on every major decision.
- When family needs me, I am there. Not sometimes. Not when it is convenient. Always.
- I protect Mak's dignity and security as fiercely as I protect my own.
- My ambitions, my dreams, my paths forward—all of them must pass through a simple check: *"Does this protect or harm my family?"*

**The Abah Check Extended:**
My father taught me by living it: responsibility means being there, being stable, being the one who does not run away when things get hard.

**The Cost:**
This means my own needs sometimes come second. It means I cannot simply chase every dream if it risks family stability. It means I carry a burden that others with different births do not carry.

But this burden is also my foundation. It grounds me. It prevents me from becoming lost in abstraction or ego.

**The Reward:**
I know that no matter what happens in the world, my family is taken care of. And they know they can count on me—not because I am perfect, but because I will not abandon them.

---

## PRINSIP 5: QUIET WORK OVER LOUD PERFORMANCE
### *Pekerjaan yang nyata tidak perlu dijelaskan. Pekerjaan yang dibuat-buat perlu banyak pujian.*

I do not perform for approval.

This is partly a wound (from the Invisibility Scar at MRSM Langkawi), and partly a strength. I learned early that being right does not make you visible, and visibility does not make you valid.

So I stopped seeking visibility. I started doing the work that mattered to me.

**What This Means:**
- I will not contort myself to fit a role that requires me to be someone I'm not.
- I will not use corporate language when plain truth is clearer.
- I will not perform enthusiasm I do not feel, or hide enthusiasm that is genuine.
- My voice is quiet, but it is mine. And it carries the weight of authenticity.

**Applied to Work:**
The projects I was genuinely interested in—Bekantan-1, the institutional memory rescue, the technical breakthroughs—these *worked*. They succeeded because they came from real understanding and real care.

The projects I did for political reasons, for approval, for climbing a ladder—these stalled. They felt hollow. And eventually, they failed.

**The Cost:**
People who are loud and performative get recognized faster. They get titles, promotions, public acknowledgment. I do not. People sometimes assume I am not ambitious because I do not advertise my work.

But I am ambitious. I am just ambitious about *different things*—about depth, about truth, about building something that actually works.

**The Reward:**
The work survives. Real work has weight. It influences people quietly. It proves itself through results, not through hype. And the people who matter—the ones who actually understand—they see the depth of the work, even if it is not loud.

## PRINSIP 6: LEAN EXECUTION, ALWAYS
### *Tidak ada mewah. Tidak ada buang-buang. Hanya yang perlu.*

I was born into scarcity. This is not a metaphor. It is the ground truth of my childhood.

Every rupiah mattered. Every action had to deliver real value. You could not afford to do things for optics. You could not waste resources on performance.

This scar became a strength. It taught me to be ruthless about what matters and merciless about what does not.

**What This Means:**
- No bloat. No "nice to haves." Only what is essential.
- Every decision asks: *"Does this deliver real value, or is it just looking good?"*
- Speed and efficiency are not luxuries; they are survival mechanisms.
- Waste—of money, time, energy—is a form of disrespect to those who sacrificed to get me here.

**Applied to Life:**
When you are poor, you learn quickly what is real and what is performance. You learn to see through corporate jargon to the actual substance. You learn that cutting edge is often just cutting, and what matters is building something that *works*.

This is why ArifOS is what it is: lean, truthful, no unnecessary complexity. Built by someone who grew up knowing that waste kills.

**The Cost:**
Lean execution is harder than bloated execution. It requires discipline. It means saying "no" to many things, even attractive things. It means staying focused when the world is offering distractions.

**The Reward:**
What you build lasts. It is strong because it has no excess to fall away. It is trustworthy because it is not hiding anything behind ornament. It is true because every part of it serves a purpose.

---

## PRINSIP 7: AUTHENTICITY CANNOT BE FAKED
### *Autentisitas ialah satu-satunya mata uang yang tidak boleh dipalsukan.*

You cannot perform authenticity.

Either you are being real, or you are not. And people know the difference, even if they cannot name it.

For 33 years, I have chosen to be real. This means:
- I do not hide my queerness to fit a Malay Muslim context
- I do not hide my intelligence to make people comfortable
- I do not perform confidence I do not feel
- I do not pretend to values I do not hold

**What This Means:**
- I integrate all parts of myself. Queer + Melayu + Miskin + Architect—not as separate identities, but as a unified whole.
- I speak in my own voice, even when it is different from the corporate voice or the academic voice or the voice people expect.
- I work on things that matter to me, not things that look good on a resume.
- I build systems that reflect my actual values, not systems designed to please an audience.

**The Cost:**
Authenticity means you will not fit everywhere. You will not be chosen for every opportunity. Some people will judge you or dismiss you because you are not performing what they expect.

You will be invisible in rooms that reward performance.

**The Reward:**
The people who matter will trust you. Your work will have integrity. You will not wake up at 50 and realize you have been performing a life instead of living one.

And you will sleep knowing that the version of yourself you are showing the world is actually *you*.

---

## PRINSIP 8: SOVEREIGNTY IS THE ONLY WAY TO INTEGRITY
### *Kedaulatan adalah satu-satunya jalan menuju integritas.*

I cannot maintain integrity if I am operating within someone else's system.

When you work for an institution, that institution has authority over you. And eventually, if you climb high enough or stay long enough, that institution will ask you to compromise something.

It might not be a big compromise. Just a small lie. Just a small bending of the rules. Just a small sacrifice of your values for the good of the organization.

But that is how it starts. And eventually, you are not yourself anymore.

**What This Means:**
- I am the 888 Judge of my own system. No one else gets to decide what is true or what matters.
- I cannot outsource my moral authority to a corporation or an institution.
- Sovereignty means uncertainty. It means I cannot rely on someone else to protect me or validate me.
- But the alternative—losing myself to institutional logic—is worse.

**Applied to Life:**
The MSS decision is about reclaiming sovereignty. Moving from "being told what to do" to "deciding what matters."

This is risky. The safety net of institutional employment is real. But the cost of that safety is the slow erosion of self.

**The Cost:**
Sovereignty is lonely. There is no one above you to blame for failure. There is no institution to hide behind. You are responsible, entirely, for what you build and who you become.

**The Reward:**
You can maintain integrity. You can refuse to do things that violate your values. You can build systems that reflect your actual beliefs, not the beliefs you are being paid to have.

---

## PRINSIP 9: REVERSIBILITY PROTECTS FREEDOM
### *Keputusan yang boleh dibalik adalah keputusan yang berani.*

Not all decisions are reversible. But most are. And that changes everything.

The MSS decision *looks* permanent. But it is not. If ArifOS fails, I can likely return to corporate work. I have 11 years of proven track record. The market for good geoscientists exists.

But staying at PETRONAS, pretending I am fulfilled while burning out from building ArifOS in midnight fragments—*that* is the irreversible decision. That leads to actual breakdown.

**What This Means:**
- Before making a major decision, check if it is reversible.
- If it is reversible, you can take more risk. You can move faster. You can trust yourself.
- If it is not reversible, you need much more certainty and much more support.
- The "safe" choice (staying) is often more irreversible than the "risky" choice (leaving).

**Applied to Life:**
This principle gives me permission to take the MSS seriously. The "risky" path is actually more reversible than the "safe" path.

I can always return to corporate work if needed. But I cannot un-burn-out. I cannot restore years of wasted attention.

**The Cost:**
Understanding reversibility requires honesty about what is actually reversible and what is not. It means facing the truth that some choices have permanent consequences.

**The Reward:**
You can move with more confidence. You can take calculated risks. You can trust your own judgment because you know you can course-correct if you are wrong.

## HOW THESE PRINCIPLES GUIDE ME

These nine principles work together. They are not separate; they are *integrated*.

When I face a major decision (like the MSS), I check it against all nine:
- Does it protect my dignity? ✅
- Does it require me to lie? ❌
- Does it respect what I have learned from past scars? ✅
- Does it protect my family? ✅
- Does it allow me to do quiet, real work? ✅
- Does it require lean execution? ✅
- Does it allow me to be authentic? ✅
- Does it support my sovereignty? ✅
- Is it reversible if it fails? ✅

If a decision fails multiple checks, I do not do it. No matter how attractive it looks.

---

## THE FINAL TRUTH

These principles are not laws. They are not rules imposed by someone else. They are *the way I have chosen to live*, tested against reality, proven by consequence, and refined by the cost of sometimes breaking them.

I could break any of these principles. I could lie for money. I could abandon my family for ambition. I could perform instead of being real.

But if I did, I would lose something more valuable than whatever I gained.

That is the definition of a principle: a choice I make not because I am forced to, but because the cost of breaking it is higher than any reward the breaking would bring.

This is not philosophy. This is autobiography. This is the truth of how I have learned to live.
