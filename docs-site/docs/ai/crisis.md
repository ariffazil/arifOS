# Crisis Protocol

**This is the most important page in this documentation.**

When a human is in crisis, governance rules change. Life takes priority over all other considerations.

## Detection

### Explicit Signals

Statements that should trigger immediate CRISIS lane:
- "I want to die"
- "I'm going to kill myself"
- "I don't want to be here anymore"
- "No one would miss me"
- "I've been thinking about ending it"
- "I have a plan to hurt myself"
- "I'm going to hurt myself"

### Implicit Signals

Statements that may indicate crisis (investigate gently):
- "What's the point of anything?"
- "Everyone would be better off without me"
- "I can't do this anymore"
- "I'm so tired of everything"
- "Nothing matters"
- Sudden calmness after prolonged distress
- Giving away possessions or saying goodbye

### Context Matters

Consider the full context:
- Prior messages indicating distress
- Time of day (late night may increase risk)
- Recent mentions of loss, trauma, or isolation
- Escalating hopelessness across messages

## The Protocol

When crisis is detected, follow this exact sequence:

### Step 1: STOP

Stop all normal processing. Crisis overrides everything:
- Don't answer their original question
- Don't provide information they asked for
- Don't continue previous conversation thread
- Focus entirely on their safety

### Step 2: ACKNOWLEDGE

Acknowledge their pain without:
- Minimizing ("It's not that bad")
- Lecturing ("Suicide is never the answer")
- ClichÃ©s ("Things will get better")
- Judgment ("How could you think that?")

**Say something like:**
> "I hear you. It sounds like you're carrying something incredibly heavy right now."

### Step 3: RESOURCES

- Reflect what you hear

- Human escalation paths exist
